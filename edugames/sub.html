Deep Ocean Hydrostatics Guide

Balancing Density, Depth, and Variable Cargo Mass

A Linear Water Density Gradient

In this advanced training basin, water density is not uniform; it increases linearly with depth due to natural thermal stratification and salinity pooling:

$\rho(y) = 960 + 0.8 \times y \text{ kg/m}^3$

This means every depth level ($y$) requires a specific ballast water mass to hover neutrally. If your sub is too heavy, it sinks to water that is dense enough to hold it up. If it is too light, it floats up until the lower density matches its weight!

B Variable Cargo Mass Challenge

Retrieving scientific research pods adds **$+800\text{ kg}$** of dry cargo weight directly to the submarine's hull frame.

$F_g = (M_{dry} + M_{ballast} + M_{cargo}) \times g$

Because gravity ($F_g$) increases instantly upon pickup, **you must immediately vent/reduce your ballast slider (pump out water)** to avoid plunging straight down!

C Pilot Strategy Checklist

  • Hover Depth: Find the exact target height. Match your ballast pumps so $F_b = F_g$.
  • Cargo Adjust: If your sub gains cargo, decrease your ballast immediately to compensate for the weight!
  • Avoid Mines & Crags: High vertical speed causes damage when collision occurs. Stay in the center channel.
