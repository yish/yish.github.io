The Weaver's Handbook of Graph Theory

A Guide to Leonhard Euler's Famous Topological Proofs

01. The Foundation: What is a Graph?

In mathematics and computer science, a graph is composed of two primary components:

  • Vertices ($V$): Often called nodes or junctions. (In our game, these are the glowing power stations).
  • Edges ($E$): The connections or bridges linking pairs of vertices. (In our game, the conduit lines).

The **Degree** of a vertex (denoted as $deg(v)$) is the total number of edges connected to it. Understanding the degree of each node is the secret to instantly unlocking any Euler puzzle.

02. The Seven Bridges of Königsberg (1736)

The city of Königsberg had seven bridges connecting two large islands to the main riverbanks. The citizens challenged each other: Can you walk across all seven bridges exactly once, returning to where you started, without retracing steps?

Leonhard Euler simplified the physical geography into a graph. He mapped the landmasses as vertices and the bridges as edges.

He realized that when you enter a landmass via a bridge, you must also leave it via a bridge. Therefore, every intermediate node must have an even degree. This laid the foundation of topology!

03. Euler Paths vs. Euler Circuits

HTML Eulerian Path

A route that visits every edge exactly once, but starts and ends at different vertices.

Requirement: Exactly 2 odd-degree vertices. All other vertices must be even.

Eulerian Circuit

An Eulerian Path that starts and ends at the exact same vertex.

Requirement: Exactly 0 odd-degree vertices. Every single node must have an even degree.

04. Fleury's Algorithm: Crucial Strategy

When solving manually, how do you prevent getting stuck? You follow Fleury's Algorithm, which says: "Do not cross a bridge unless there are no other options."

A bridge (or cut-edge) is an edge that, if removed, disconnects the remaining graph into separate, isolated sub-graphs. If you cross a bridge before clearing the sector you are currently in, you are locked out of those remaining lines permanently!
